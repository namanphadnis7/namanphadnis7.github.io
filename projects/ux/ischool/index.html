iSchool Identity Research

This was a collaborative group project for our client, the College of Information Studies at the University of Maryland.


We conducted research with students in the department of Information Science to get insights into the program and provide solutions to improve it for the students.

The Problem

The client wanted to promote their Information Science program in a better way for current and prospective students.


Trends showed that many people did not know about the existence of the program itself, and a significant portion of the students were transferred from other departments.The college wanted to work on creating a unique identity for the program.

Research Methods

User Interviews

We organized six contextual interviews with students with the goal of consistent yet diverse data. We picked interviewees of various genders, ethnicity and majors instead of picking only InfoSci students to get more insight on the views of students outside the program.

Interpretation Sessions

Our team held interpretation sessions after the interviews to fully understand their context. We asked the interviewers questions and discussed the information we might have missed out on during the interviews for follow-up questions.

Analyzing our data

Contextual Models

Identity Model

We crafted some identities of the students in and around the program based on the sentimental data from the affinity diagram. The identities were divided into three sections, based on who they were, what they wanted, and what they liked.


We also gave each identity a 'Give Me' section, coming up with what this identity would want to improve their experience in the iSchool.

Relationship Model

We built the relationship model to outline the internal and external relationships of the students with various aspects of the program and beyond.


We divided this into three sections as well with increasing levels of scope, their relationship with themselves, their program, and the rest of the world, which provided a large overview of their decision-making process within the program.
