Healthy Promotion of E-Sports


I created prototypes for improving the promotion of e-sports among youth, specifically young adults- both in terms of promotion parallel to traditional sports, alongside health and self-care.


Objective

To discover gaps in the existing ways esports is promoted among youth and improve them, and spread awareness of the importance of well-being in gaming alongside.

Role

• UX Researcher

• Co-Designer with Youth

Challenges

• Prompting the participants for qualitative and detailed opinions.


• Creating feasible solutions from the participant data.

Final Result

Medium-Fidelity Prototypes

How Might We?

To understand the problem space better, I framed some "How Might We?" questions. Asking these questions to myself resulted in a lot of issues emerging in the world of esports which I wanted to improve.


I narrowed these questions down to a limited scope to keep my project viable and then worked on finding an answer through design.

Research Methods

User Interviews

To understand the problem space better, I framed some "How Might We?" questions. Asking these questions to myself resulted in a lot of issues emerging in the world of esports which I wanted to improve.


I narrowed these questions down to a limited scope to keep my project viable and then worked on finding an answer through design.

Participatory Design Sessions

I did two interviews with gamers between the ages of 17-19 to get detailed insights into the existing problems in esports.


I divided my interview questions into four categories: Questions about them, esports, promotion, and what their parents thought about it.

First Drafts

Ideation and Sketches

The design process started with coming up with potential solutions and drafting rough sketches of them to get the ideas out on paper.


Many solutions were discarded because of viability, time constraints, and scope of the project until I had three solutions I was satisfied with.

Initial Prototypes

I started the process of converting my sketches to digital prototypes and rough mockups using Figma.


I crafted three initial prototypes, which were later improved upon during the iteration phase.

Final Deliverables

Newbie Friendly Information System

Almost all youtube videos are labeled at the bottom of the video with the name of the game.


I created an overlay button similar to the subscribe overlay for new players to click, which would direct them to a newbie friendly informatics website for the labeled game.

The landing page would ask them how familiar they are with gaming in general. Based on their selection, it would take them to an informatics page which would break the game down for them based on their experience with games.

The informatic pages would explain the game in a way that the user would understand whether they are a veteran gamer or have no idea about games.


This idea was inspired by me wishing there existed some medium for me to show my non-gamer friends when they watched my esports matches. No more confusion of what's happening on the screen!

In-Game Menu with Athletics-like Promotion

I added an additional menu to the main menu of competitive video games. This "Guides" menu would lead to an in-game menu with various resources to help out players.

The menu would consist of various articles, videos, tips, and guides for well-being as a player and in general as well.


The resources would be created by pros and veterans of the game for the overall development of the user not just as a player, but as a person.
