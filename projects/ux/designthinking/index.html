Design Thinking for Youth

I created prototypes for improving the promotion of e-sports among youth, specifically young adults- both in terms of promotion parallel to traditional sports, alongside health and self-care.

How might we?

To understand the problem space better, I framed some "How Might We?" questions. Asking these questions to myself resulted in a lot of issues emerging in the world of esports which I wanted to improve. I narrowed these questions down to a limited scope to keep my project viable and then worked on finding an answer through design.

Research Methods

User Interviews

I did two interviews with gamers between the ages of 17-19 to get detailed insights into the existing problems in esports. I divided my interview questions into four categories: Questions about them, esports, promotion, and what their parents thought about it.

Participatory Design Sessions

A participatory design session was conducted as a follow-up to the interviews. The participants followed Layered Elaboration method using Google Jamboard to further elaborate on the issues and good things in the current state of esports.

Rough Drafts

Ideation and Sketches

The design process started with coming up with potential solutions and drafting rough sketches of them to get the ideas out on paper. Many solutions were discarded because of viability, time constraints, and scope of the project until I had three solutions I was satisfied with.

Initial Prototypes

I started the process of converting my sketches to digital prototypes and rough mockups using Figma. I crafted three initial prototypes, which were later improved upon during the iteration phase.

Final Prototypes

Prototype#1: Newbie Friendly Informatics

Almost all youtube videos are labeled at the bottom of the video with the name of the game. I created an overlay button similar to the subscribe overlay for new players to click, which would direct them to a newbie friendly informatics website.

The landing page would ask them how familiar they are with gaming in general. Based on their selection, it would take them to an informatics page which would break the game down for them based on their experience with games.

The informatic pages would explain the game in a way that the user would understand whether they are a veteran gamer or have no idea about games. This idea was inspired by me wishing there existed some medium for me to show my non-gamer friends when they watched my esports matches. No more confusion of what's happening on the screen!

Prototype#2: Expansion on Other Platforms

I created mockups to show expansion of the promotion of esports outside game-specific platforms. This isn't limited to one idea but many- ads on mainstream tv, news articles with esports section inside the sports section, Netflix documentaries, more esports involvement in in-person events and college festivals, etc.

Prototype#3: Promotion Parallel to Athletics In-game

I added an additional menu to the main menu of competitive video games. This "Guides" menu would lead to an in-game menu with various resources to help out players.

The menu would consist of various articles, videos, tips, and guides for well-being as a player and in general as well. The resources would be created by pros and veterans of the game for the overall development of the user not just as a player, but as a person.
