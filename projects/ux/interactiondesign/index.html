Interaction Design Project

I designed a small-scale app concept for a limited audience using wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes. I also created personas of the user to make contextual improvements and the user journey of this persona throughout the day with the usage of this app.

The app I designed was "UMD Overwatch", centralizing the activities of the university's esports team in one place- from group chat to match schedules and player profiles. I chose to design an app for them as they were the perfect target users for a small scale app alongside my personal love for them. The team also uses several different apps for their activities, and this app would save a lot of time and effort juggling between these apps.

Envisioning the app

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Based on the initial sketches and mockups, I created a low-fidelity prototype in Figma, showing how the app navigation would look after its component design.

Component Library

I designed individual components for my app's wireframes. For the colors, I chose the University's colors to represent the team, modifying them slightly to fit the overall modern yet athletics-like feel of the app. I chose bold and strong fonts that are usually associated with traditional sports.

High-Fidelity Prototype

I used the components as building blocks to build a wireframe, just like assembling lego pieces. After I did so, I created a high-fidelity prototype using the wireframe screens to demonstrate how navigating the actual app will look like.

User Persona

I created a user persona based on the common personality traits and patterns of members of the UMD Overwatch team. This helped me understand my target users better and make contextual improvements to the app in the iterative cycles
