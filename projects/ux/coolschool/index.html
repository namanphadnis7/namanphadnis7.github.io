Cool School Re-Design


Objective

To create a prototype in Figma for an existing children's educational game with a more modern UI and game elements.


Improving outdated materials to keep up with the current gaming and social culture.

Role

• UX Designer

• Digital Artist

• Video Editor


Group project with 4 members in the team.

Challenges

• Prompting constructive feedback from the kids during the testing phase (they wanted to see more fights!)


• Ignoring general design principles to create a colorful and vibrant experience for 5-7 year olds.

Sprint 1

Replayability

First Draft

I created the first set of screens to give a base structure to the game. This included on-screen icons, the first set of screens, and basic game assets.

Integrating Replayability

I studied competitors and other video games to see how they ethically implemented replayability. I added elements in the game such as item shop, character customization, achievements, and map exploration.

Sprint 2

Content and Fluid Gameplay

Making it feel real

To bring the game to life, I drew a set of game assets and imported them to vectors, which made it easy to customize characters. Using these, I created animations and added audio to make the prototype feel closer to an actual game.

Modern, but original

To keep the original magic of cool school, I created cutscenes from their original scripts; but with the characters modernized into hand drawn shape doodles. Doing this would ensure the characters aren't limited to things like ethnicity or gender- just cute waddly creatures.

Sprint 3

Open World Experience

Creating a new world

I didn't want the game to feel like a linear storyline, and give players a sense of freedom to explore and find conflicts. For this, I created a detailed map, and added multiple rooms and areas within each location.

Navigation and Interactions

To give the players a sense of control, I let them move between buildings with their avatar. This also presented the opportunity to add more cute interactions on the way!

Sprint 4

Customizations

Character Customization

I polished the screens from Sprint 1, along with adding new features such as voice customization, randomize avatar button, animal sounds, and more variety for color options.

Item Customization

To further give players a sense of identity and originality, I also let players customize the in-game accessories that they obtained in the game.

Design Rewamps

I also polished screens from the previous prototypes such as the Shop and the Achievements screens, and the UI on the game screen and added some new screens such as Player Profile.
