Mental Health Website

Mental health has always been very important to me, and the life of a graduate student can be stressful. Therefore, my classmate and I worked together to create a website for our HCIM cohort, with various helpful resources and solutions to help someone going through mental health problems.

Design Process

The process started with an ideation meeting where we decided the features, viability, and the theme of the website. We wanted the website to feel welcoming and safe, and give the users as much resources as possible for their well being in one place.

We started by selecting our color scheme and typography. A calm and neutral light blue with some gentle pink felt like the best fit for a mental health website.

We began the wireframing process in Figma to decide the layout and look of our website before we began coding.

After finalizing the look of the website, we began a collaborative coding session over discord where we converted the website from a wireframe to a live one. We added a mini-game on the website for users to play to de-stress, and removed some features due to time constraints of the project.
