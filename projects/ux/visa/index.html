Jump to Result  

I took part in a 24hr UX design 'Makeathon' with a friend, organized by the University of Maryland's UXTerps organization. We had come up with a solution for a design challenge issued to us by Visa, along with showing the whole process and our reasoning behind it. We won 2nd place!


The challenge was to make the transition to digital payment systems for small businesses owned by minorities easier.

How Might We?

After our research from Literature Review, we framed HMW questions to understand our problem space and objective.


We brainstormed solutions for the existing problems and began our design phase.

Rough Sketches

We sketched and doodled all possible ideas, regardless of how good or bad to brainstorm as many as possible.

Draft Prototypes

We created rough prototypes to get an idea of where we wanted to go with the solutions and the design of the app.

Designing the Solution

Theme and Colors

We decided on color patterns and fonts similar to banking and visa themes, but with a mix of calming baby pink to generate a sense of ease and comfort.

The Community and Blog

We created a community section in our app where people could discuss topics, ask others for help and connect with other minority-owned small business owners.


They would get recommendations based on the questions they answer on the app's first launch. We also created a blog section containing helpful articles related to business or their communities.

The Steps and The Store

On our home page, we broke down the exact steps for the transition to digital payment systems in one place, in a way that won't be hard for the users to understand.


We also created a store section so if the users needed to purchase any device for this process, they wouldn't have to go anywhere else.
