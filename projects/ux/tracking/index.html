Find My Stuff: Object Tracking for the Elderly


Objective

To design a unique solution for our participant, catering to their specific needs and exclusion from technology.

Role

• UX Researcher

• UX Designer

• Video Editor


This was a group project with a team of 3 members.

Challenges

• To create solutions that can be implemented practically with modern technologies.


• To ensure that the created solution meets the participant's satisfaction and successfully solves their problem.

Final Result

Introductory User Interview

Our group had an initial interview with the participant to get a deep understanding of their everyday problems with technology. The interview was very informal and more of a conversation than a structured format.

This helped us gain qualititative insights and understand the key problem areas. We looked at our notes on what the participant said and identified three major themes.


We interpreted finding everyday objects to be the participant's biggest concern, and decided to make that our project focus.

Ideation and Early Prototypes

After deciding our project focus, we drafted sketches of ideas and created storyboards. We showed these to our participant to ensure we were heading in the right direction.

Once we confirmed the participant was happy with the ideas, we created early low-fi prototypes to get our ideas on a screen.

Co-Designing the Final Version

After making some draft screens, we had a co-design session with the participant where we designed the app with them. They told us what they liked and disliked, and gave us additional ideas for features.

With their help, we refined the app to its final version- a high fidelity prototype with all the requested features and addressing as many problems from the initial interview as possible.
