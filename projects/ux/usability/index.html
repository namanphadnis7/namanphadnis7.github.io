Usability Testing

I tested the Overwatch League's offcial website for its usability through user-based testing as well as heuristic evaluations. I then compared the data from both tests to find problem trends and came up with solutions to overcome them.

User Based Testing

User Tasks

After taking their consent and giving them instructions, I gave the testers some tasks with mixed difficulties to do across the website, noting how easy they found it to be and their time performance while doing it. The users were also encouraged to use their mics to point out anything that stood out to them during the process.

Performance Analysis

All of the data collected from the observations was analyzed together to find common trends like the most difficult task, the longest task, and what was easy to navigate. I used the TryMyUI app to conduct these tests, and analyzed the auto generated SUS report for these trends.

Heuristic Evaluations

I used Schneiderman's 8 golden rules of interface design to evaluate the Overwatch League website as an expert. Using the principles I learnt in my HCI fundamentals class and the golden rules, I listed the problems I found within the website.

Performance Analysis

Some of them were unique while some matched with the problems from the user-based tests. Comparing and contrasting the two methods backed up some of the data as well as led to discoveries about the variation in usability based on familiarity and experience with technology and design.
